## 5. Budget Optimization Rules

### When to Increase Budget

#### Scale-Up Triggers
- **ROAS > 300%** for 2+ weeks → Scale by 30-50%
- **CPA < 350K** for 2+ weeks → Scale by 20-30%
- **CTR > 3%** (Facebook) or **> 6%** (Google) → Scale creative
- **Lead quality > 70%** → Scale lead gen campaigns

#### Scaling Limits
- **Week 1-2:** Max +30% per week (test period)
- **Week 3-4:** Max +50% per week (gaining confidence)
- **Month 2+:** Max +100% per week (proven winner)

#### Budget Cap
- **Single campaign:** Max 15M/month (avoid fatigue)
- **Single ad set:** Max 5M/month
- **Single platform:** Max 35M/month (diversify risk)

### When to Decrease Budget

#### Scale-Down Triggers
- **ROAS < 200%** for 2+ weeks → Cut by 50%
- **CPA > 600K** for 2+ weeks → Cut by 30%
- **CTR < 1%** for 2+ weeks → Pause or refresh
- **Lead quality < 40%** → Reconsider targeting

#### Pause Criteria
- **3 consecutive weeks** below target KPIs → Pause
- **Negative ROI** (ROAS < 100%) → Immediate pause
- **Technical issues** (tracking broken) → Pause until fixed
- **Seasonal downtime** (Tết) → Reduce, don't pause

## 6. Budget Reallocation Strategy

### Monthly Reallocation Process

#### Week 1: Analyze Last Month
- Identify top 20% campaigns (by ROAS)
- Identify bottom 20% campaigns (by ROAS)
- Calculate opportunity cost of underperformers

#### Week 2: Plan Reallocation
- **From bottom 20%:** Cut budget by 50-100%
- **To top 20%:** Add 30-50% more budget
- **Hold middle 60%:** Maintain current levels

#### Week 3: Execute Changes
- Implement budget shifts on Monday
- Monitor daily for 3 days
- Adjust if performance changes unexpectedly

#### Week 4: Review & Document
- Document what worked and what didn't
- Update budget rules based on learnings
- Plan next month's strategy

### Reallocation Example

**Before (Month 3):**
```
Campaign A (ROAS 380%): 5M
```

## 9. ROI & Profitability Analysis

### Revenue Calculation

#### Average Revenue per Customer
- **Day Pass:** 50K VND (one-time)
- **Week Pass:** 250K VND (one-time)
- **Month Pass:** 800K VND × 3 months = 2.4M VND (avg. 3-month stay)
- **Private Office:** 3M VND × 6 months = 18M VND (avg. 6-month stay)
- **Weighted Avg:** 3.5M VND/customer

#### ROI Formula
```
ROI = (Revenue - Ad Spend) / Ad Spend × 100%

Example:
Revenue = 50 customers × 3.5M = 175M
Ad Spend = 40M
ROI = (175M - 40M) / 40M × 100% = 337.5%
```

#### LTV:CAC Ratio
```
LTV:CAC = Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost

Target: LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1
Current: 3.5M / 0.42M = 8.3:1 ✅ Excellent
```

### Break-Even Analysis

#### Break-Even CPA
```
Break-Even CPA = Avg. Revenue / Target ROAS

Example:
Avg. Revenue = 3.5M
Target ROAS = 250%
Break-Even CPA = 3.5M / 2.5 = 1.4M

Current CPA = 420K ✅ Well below break-even
```

#### Maximum Acceptable CPA
```
Max CPA = (Revenue - COGS - Operating Costs) / Min. Margin

Revenue = 3.5M
COGS = 500K (cafe, utilities)
OpEx = 800K (staff, rent allocation)
Min. Margin = 20%
Max CPA = (3.5M - 0.5M - 0.8M) × 0.8 = 1.76M

Current CPA = 420K ✅ Comfortable margin
```

## 10. Budget Monitoring & Control

### Daily Budget Checks

#### Morning Checklist (9:00 AM)
- [ ] Yesterday's spend within 10% of daily budget
- [ ] No campaigns paused unexpectedly
- [ ] No payment issues or billing alerts
- [ ] Conversion tracking firing correctly

#### Afternoon Review (3:00 PM)
- [ ] Daily pacing on track (33%, 67%, 100% of month)
- [ ] Top 5 campaigns performing well
- [ ] No CPA spikes (> 600K) for 2+ days
- [ ] No budget exhausted campaigns

#### Weekly Budget Review (Friday PM)
- [ ] Weekly spend vs. budget variance < 5%
- [ ] Monthly pacing on track (25%, 50%, 75%, 100%)
- [ ] Top 3 and bottom 3 campaigns identified
- [ ] Budget adjustments planned for next week

### Alert Thresholds

#### Spend Alerts
- **Daily spend > 2M** → Check for fraud/error
- **Weekly spend > 15M** → Verify pacing
- **Monthly spend variance > 10%** → Investigate

#### Performance Alerts
- **CPA > 600K for 3 days** → Review campaign
- **ROAS < 200% for 1 week** → Cut budget 50%
- **Conversions < 5 for 1 week** → Check tracking

#### Budget Exhaustion Alerts
- **Campaign budget exhausted** → Auto-pause, notify
- **Account budget low** → Replenish before 0
