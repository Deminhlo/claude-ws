## 5. KPI Definitions & Benchmarks

### 5.1 Core KPIs

#### Awareness Metrics
- **Impressions:** Total ad views
- **Reach:** Unique people reached
- **Frequency:** Avg. times person saw ad
- **CPM:** Cost per 1,000 impressions
- **Benchmark:** CPM ≤ 30K (Facebook), ≤ 50K (Google Display)

#### Engagement Metrics
- **CTR:** Click-through rate (clicks/impressions)
- **CPC:** Cost per click
- **Landing Page View Rate:** LP views/clicks
- **Engagement Rate:** (likes+comments+shares)/impressions
- **Benchmark:** CTR ≥ 2% (Facebook), ≥ 4% (Google Search)

#### Lead Metrics
- **Leads:** Total form submissions/calls
- **CPL:** Cost per lead
- **Lead Quality:** % qualified leads
- **Lead-to-Opportunity:** % leads → opportunities
- **Benchmark:** CPL ≤ 200K, Quality ≥ 60%

#### Conversion Metrics
- **Conversions:** Trial signups + memberships
- **CPA:** Cost per acquisition
- **Conversion Rate:** Conversions/clicks
- **ROAS:** Revenue / Ad spend
- **Benchmark:** CPA ≤ 500K, ROAS ≥ 250%

#### Retention Metrics
- **Trial-to-Paid:** % trials → paid memberships
- **Churn Rate:** % customers cancel/month
- **LTV:** Lifetime value per customer
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** LTV / CPA
- **Benchmark:** LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1

### 5.2 Secondary KPIs

#### Website Metrics (Google Analytics)
- **Bounce Rate:** % single-page sessions (< 70%)
- **Avg. Session Duration:** Time on site (> 2 min)
- **Pages per Session:** Depth of engagement (> 2 pages)
- **Return User Rate:** % returning visitors (> 30%)

#### Social Media Metrics
- **Follower Growth Rate:** % new followers/month (> 5%)
- **Engagement Rate:** (likes+comments+shares)/followers (> 5%)
- **Share of Voice:** Mentions vs. competitors
- **Sentiment Score:** Positive vs. negative mentions (> 80% positive)

#### Customer Metrics
- **NPS Score:** Net Promoter Score (> 50)
- **Customer Satisfaction:** Avg. rating (> 4.5/5)
- **Referral Rate:** % customers who refer (> 20%)
- **Repeat Purchase Rate:** % customers renew (> 60%)

## 6. Reporting Best Practices

### 6.1 Data Quality Checklist

#### Before Sending Reports
- [ ] Data covers full reporting period
- [ ] No data gaps or missing days
- [ ] Attribution model is consistent
- [ ] Currency conversions correct (if applicable)
- [ ] Duplicate conversions removed
- [ ] Bot traffic filtered out
- [ ] All calculations verified
- [ ] Visualizations accurate
- [ ] Spelling and grammar checked
- [ ] Action items clear and specific

### 6.2 Storytelling with Data

#### Narrative Structure
1. **Context:** What happened (the numbers)
2. **Causation:** Why it happened (the analysis)
3. **Implication:** What it means (the impact)
4. **Action:** What to do next (the plan)

**Example:**
"**What:** Facebook CPA increased 15% this month (512K → 589K)
**Why:** New ad creative underperformed (-22% CTR), increased competition in coworking niche
**So What:** We're spending 77K more per acquisition, reducing overall margin by 3%
**Now What:** Pause underperforming ads, return to proven creative, test 2 new variations next week"

### 6.3 Visualization Best Practices

#### Do's ✅
- Use one message per chart
- Choose right chart type (trends = line, comparison = bar, parts = pie)
- Label axes and data points clearly
- Use consistent color scheme
- Highlight key numbers (bold, color)
- Include benchmark/target lines
- Keep it simple (less is more)

#### Don'ts ❌
- Don't clutter with too much data
- Don't use 3D effects (distorts data)
- Don't use misleading scales
- Don't mix units (e.g., VND and USD)
- Don't use too many colors (max 5)
- Don't make audience do the math (show % change)
- Don't hide the headline message
